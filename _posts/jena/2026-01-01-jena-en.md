Jena is a small town located in Thuringia, central Germany. Despite its size, it is a significant technological hub, renowned for its optical industry (Carl Zeiss and Schott Glassworks), as well as research in microbiology and ecology (Max Planck Institute for Chemical Ecology).

The River Saale flows through Jena from south to north. As a result, the houses on the slopes of the valley enjoy magnificent views and have magical forests as their backyards. Beyond Jena's own natural resources and history, there are many interesting attractions in the surrounding areas worth visiting!

## History of Jena
The history of Jena can be traced back to between 830 and 850 AD, when the city was first mentioned in the Hersfeld Tithe Register (Hersfelder Zehntverzeichnis). In 1140, following the death of William IV (Wilhelm von Ballenstedt) of the House of Ascania (Askanier), the Lords of Lobdeburg (Herren von Lobdeburg) from the House of Auhausen became the owners of Jena and elevated it to city status in 1230.

Lobdeburg itself was first mentioned in 1166. The castle was completed in 1185 and became the residence of the Lobdeburg family. Over the following centuries, the influence of the House of Lobdeburg gradually declined until 1423, when Jena was incorporated into the Electorate of Saxony.

The most well-known chapter of Jena's history is perhaps the Battle of Jena–Auerstedt (Schlacht bei Jena und Auerstedt) on October 14, 1806, where the Grande Armée commanded by Napoléon Bonaparte defeated the Prussian Army. This victory completely crushed the Prussian military power. To commemorate the triumph, Napoléon even built a bridge named after the battle in Paris. Today, information boards detailing the battle can still be seen in the southern part of Jena.

The University of Jena itself is also steeped in history. Founded in 1558, it is one of Germany’s ten oldest universities and remains a vital cultural and educational institution for the city today.

Also worth mentioning is the JenTower, which began construction in 1969 and was completed in 1972. It was the tallest office building in East Germany and remains the tallest in the state of Thuringia, as well as the 28th tallest building in Germany.

## Mönchsberg
At 399 meters above sea level, Mönchsberg is an excellent spot to overlook Jena and a popular destination for weekend excursions. From 1886 to 1967, limestone was quarried here; later, it served as a shooting range and briefly as a car cemetery.

Today, at the foot of the hill, you can find many stone labyrinths and letters created by visitors.
